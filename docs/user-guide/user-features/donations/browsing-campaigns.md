# Browsing Campaigns

Learn how to browse, search, and filter donation campaigns in the NORSU Alumni System.

## Overview

The donation campaigns page allows you to discover fundraising initiatives, view campaign details, and find causes you want to support. You can browse all active campaigns, filter by type, search for specific campaigns, and sort results to find the most relevant opportunities.

## Who Can Use This Feature

- **Public Users**: Can view campaigns marked as "Public"
- **Registered Alumni**: Can view both "Public" and "Registered Alumni" campaigns

## How to Access

### For Public Users
1. Visit the NORSU Alumni System website
2. Navigate to **Donations** or **Campaigns** from the main menu
3. You'll see all publicly available campaigns

### For Registered Alumni
1. Log in to your account
2. Click **Donations** in the navigation menu
3. Select **Browse Campaigns** or **Campaigns**
4. You'll see all campaigns you have access to

## Browsing Campaigns

### Campaign List View

The campaign list displays active campaigns in a grid or list format. Each campaign card shows:

- **Campaign Image**: Featured image representing the campaign
- **Campaign Name**: Title of the fundraising initiative
- **Short Description**: Brief overview of the campaign purpose
- **Progress Bar**: Visual indicator of fundraising progress
- **Amount Raised**: Current amount donated vs. goal amount
- **Progress Percentage**: Percentage of goal achieved
- **Days Remaining**: Time left until campaign ends (if applicable)
- **Donor Count**: Number of people who have donated
- **Campaign Type**: Category badge (e.g., Scholarship, Infrastructure, Event)
- **Status Badge**: Current campaign status (Active, Featured, etc.)

### Campaign Status Indicators

- **Active**: Campaign is currently accepting donations
- **Featured**: Highlighted campaign promoted by administrators
- **Ending Soon**: Campaign approaching its end date
- **Public/Registered**: Visibility indicator

## Searching for Campaigns

Use the search bar to find specific campaigns:

1. Locate the **Search** box at the top of the campaigns page
2. Enter keywords related to:
   - Campaign name
   - Description content
   - Beneficiaries
   - Purpose or goals
3. Press **Enter** or click the **Search** button
4. Results will update to show matching campaigns

**Search Tips**:
- Use specific keywords for better results
- Try different terms if you don't find what you're looking for
- Search is case-insensitive
- Partial matches are supported

## Filtering Campaigns

### Filter by Campaign Type

Filter campaigns by their category:

1. Look for the **Campaign Type** dropdown or sidebar
2. Select a type from the available options:
   - Scholarship Fund
   - Infrastructure Development
   - Event Funding
   - Emergency Relief
   - Alumni Projects
   - General Fund
   - (Other types as configured)
3. The list will update to show only campaigns of that type
4. Select **All Types** to clear the filter

### Filter by Status

Filter campaigns by their current status:

1. Find the **Status** filter dropdown
2. Choose from:
   - **All Statuses**: Show all campaigns
   - **Active**: Currently accepting donations
   - **Draft**: Not yet published (admin only)
   - **Paused**: Temporarily suspended
   - **Completed**: Goal reached or ended
   - **Cancelled**: No longer active
3. The list updates automatically

### Filter by Visibility

Filter based on who can view the campaign:

1. Locate the **Visibility** filter
2. Select:
   - **All Visibility**: Show all accessible campaigns
   - **Public**: Campaigns visible to everyone
   - **Registered Alumni**: Campaigns for registered users only
3. Results update based on your selection

**Note**: Public users can only see public campaigns regardless of filter selection.

## Sorting Campaigns

Change the order in which campaigns are displayed:

1. Find the **Sort By** dropdown
2. Choose your preferred sorting option:
   - **Most Recent**: Newest campaigns first (default)
   - **Ending Soon**: Campaigns closest to their end date
   - **Most Funded**: Highest donation amounts first
   - **Largest Goal**: Campaigns with biggest fundraising targets
3. The campaign list reorders automatically

## Viewing Campaign Details

To see full details about a campaign:

1. Click on any campaign card or the **View Details** button
2. You'll be taken to the campaign detail page showing:
   - Complete campaign description
   - Full progress information
   - Recent donors list (non-anonymous)
   - Campaign updates and news
   - Donation form (if accepting donations)
   - Campaign timeline
   - Beneficiary information

See [Making Donations](making-donations.md) for information on how to donate to a campaign.

## Campaign Sidebar Information

The sidebar (if available) provides quick access to:

- **Campaign Types**: List of all types with campaign counts
- **Quick Filters**: One-click filtering options
- **Featured Campaigns**: Highlighted initiatives
- **Recent Activity**: Latest donations or updates

## Understanding Campaign Progress

### Progress Bar
- **Green**: Campaign is progressing well
- **Yellow**: Approaching goal
- **Blue**: Goal exceeded (if applicable)

### Progress Percentage
- Shows what percentage of the goal has been reached
- Calculated as: (Current Amount / Goal Amount) × 100
- Capped at 100% in most displays

### Days Remaining
- Shows countdown to campaign end date
- "No end date" if campaign is ongoing
- "Ended" if past the end date

## Tips for Finding Campaigns

1. **Use Filters Together**: Combine type, status, and sorting for precise results
2. **Check Featured Campaigns**: These are often high-priority initiatives
3. **Look for Ending Soon**: Support campaigns that need immediate help
4. **Browse by Type**: Find causes that align with your interests
5. **Read Descriptions**: Click through to learn more before donating

## Campaign Types Explained

Common campaign types you might encounter:

- **Scholarship Fund**: Support student education and financial aid
- **Infrastructure Development**: Building and facility improvements
- **Event Funding**: Support for alumni events and gatherings
- **Emergency Relief**: Urgent assistance for disasters or crises
- **Alumni Projects**: Community initiatives led by alumni
- **General Fund**: Flexible funding for various needs

## Pagination

When there are many campaigns:

1. Results are displayed in pages (typically 9 campaigns per page)
2. Use the **pagination controls** at the bottom:
   - Click page numbers to jump to specific pages
   - Use **Previous** and **Next** buttons to navigate
   - Current page is highlighted
3. Your filters and search remain active across pages

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why can't I see all campaigns?**  
A: Some campaigns are restricted to registered alumni only. Create an account and log in to see all available campaigns.

**Q: How do I know if a campaign is legitimate?**  
A: All campaigns are created and verified by system administrators. Look for the campaign creator information and official badges.

**Q: Can I donate to multiple campaigns?**  
A: Yes! You can donate to as many campaigns as you like. Each donation is processed separately.

**Q: What if a campaign has ended?**  
A: Ended campaigns are typically hidden from the main list. You can view them in your donation history if you previously donated.

**Q: How often is the progress updated?**  
A: Campaign progress updates in real-time as donations are verified and completed by administrators.
