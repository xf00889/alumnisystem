# Browsing Jobs

## Overview

The Job Board allows you to discover and explore employment opportunities from NORSU and external organizations. You can browse active job listings, search for specific positions, filter by various criteria, and use skill-based matching to find jobs that align with your qualifications. The system provides both internal NORSU positions and external opportunities from partner organizations.

## Who Can Use This Feature

- **User Role**: All authenticated alumni users
- **Permissions Required**: Active user account
- **Prerequisites**: 
  - Must be logged in to the system
  - For skill-based matching: Complete profile with skills, education, and experience

## How to Access

1. Log in to your alumni account
2. Click on **"Jobs"** or **"Career Opportunities"** in the main navigation menu
3. You will be directed to the Job Board page

## Key Features

- View all active job postings
- Search jobs by title, company, location, or description
- Filter by job type (Full Time, Part Time, Contract, Remote, Internship)
- Filter by source type (NORSU Internal, External Organization)
- Sort by posting date (newest or oldest first)
- View featured job listings
- Enable skill-based job matching
- View job match scores based on your profile
- Get skill recommendations to improve job matches
- Access external job links
- View job details in modal windows

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Task 1: Viewing the Job List

The Job Board displays all active job postings with key information at a glance.

1. **Navigate to the Job Board**
   - Click on **"Jobs"** in the main navigation menu
   - **Expected Result**: You will see the Job List page with available positions

2. **Review the job listings**
   - Each job card displays:
     - Job title
     - Company name
     - Location
     - Job type (Full Time, Part Time, etc.)
     - Posted date
     - Source type (Internal or External)
     - Brief description
     - Featured badge (if applicable)
   - **Expected Result**: You can quickly scan multiple job opportunities

3. **View featured jobs**
   - Featured jobs appear at the top or in a special section
   - These are highlighted positions of particular importance
   - **Expected Result**: Priority jobs are easily identifiable

4. **Navigate through pages**
   - If there are more than 10 jobs, use pagination controls at the bottom
   - Click page numbers or "Next"/"Previous" buttons to browse more listings
   - **Expected Result**: The page loads the next set of job postings

### Task 2: Searching for Jobs

Use the search function to find specific job opportunities.

1. **Locate the search box**
   - On the Job List page, find the search input field
   - It's typically located at the top of the page
   - **Expected Result**: Search box is visible and ready for input

2. **Enter your search query**
   - Type keywords related to the job you're looking for
   - You can search by:
     - Job title (e.g., "Software Engineer", "Marketing Manager")
     - Company name (e.g., "NORSU", "Tech Corp")
     - Location (e.g., "Dumaguete", "Remote")
     - Job description content
   - **Expected Result**: Search terms are entered in the search box

3. **Execute the search**
   - Press **Enter** or click the **Search** button
   - **Expected Result**: The page refreshes and displays only jobs matching your search terms

4. **Review search results**
   - Jobs matching your query are displayed
   - The search looks across multiple fields for comprehensive results
   - **Expected Result**: You see a filtered list of relevant job opportunities

5. **Clear the search**
   - To return to viewing all jobs, clear the search box
   - Delete your search terms and press Enter, or click a "Clear" button
   - **Expected Result**: All active jobs are displayed again

### Task 3: Filtering Jobs by Type

Filter jobs based on employment type to find positions that match your availability.

1. **Locate the job type filter**
   - On the Job List page, find the "Job Type" filter dropdown or buttons
   - **Expected Result**: Filter controls are visible

2. **Select a job type**
   - Choose from available types:
     - **Full Time**: Traditional full-time employment
     - **Part Time**: Part-time positions
     - **Contract**: Contract or project-based work
     - **Remote**: Remote work opportunities
     - **Internship**: Internship positions
   - Click on your desired job type
   - **Expected Result**: The job list updates to show only positions of the selected type

3. **Clear the filter**
   - To view all job types again, clear the filter
   - Look for a "Clear" or "All" option
   - **Expected Result**: All jobs are displayed regardless of type

### Task 4: Filtering Jobs by Source

Filter jobs based on whether they're internal NORSU positions or external opportunities.

1. **Locate the source type filter**
   - Find the "Source Type" filter on the Job List page
   - **Expected Result**: Source filter controls are visible

2. **Select a source type**
   - Choose from:
     - **NORSU Internal**: Positions within NORSU or the alumni network that accept applications through the system
     - **External Organization**: Opportunities from external companies with links to their application pages
   - Click on your desired source type
   - **Expected Result**: The job list updates to show only jobs from the selected source

3. **Understand the difference**
   - Internal jobs allow you to apply directly through the system
   - External jobs redirect you to the organization's application page
   - **Expected Result**: You know which jobs you can apply for internally

### Task 5: Sorting Jobs

Change the order in which jobs are displayed.

1. **Locate the sort options**
   - Find the "Sort by" dropdown on the Job List page
   - **Expected Result**: Sort controls are visible

2. **Select a sort order**
   - Choose from:
     - **Newest First** (default): Most recently posted jobs appear first
     - **Oldest First**: Older job postings appear first
   - Click on your preferred sort option
   - **Expected Result**: The job list reorders according to your selection

3. **Review the sorted list**
   - Jobs are now displayed in your chosen order
   - **Expected Result**: You can browse jobs in your preferred sequence

### Task 6: Using Skill-Based Job Matching

Enable skill-based matching to see jobs that align with your qualifications and get personalized match scores.

1. **Ensure your profile is complete**
   - Before using skill matching, make sure you have:
     - Added your skills in your profile
     - Completed your education history
     - Added your work experience
   - **Expected Result**: Your profile contains the information needed for matching

2. **Enable skill-based view**
   - On the Job List page, find the "Skill-Based View" toggle or checkbox
   - Click to enable skill-based matching
   - **Expected Result**: The toggle is activated and the page refreshes

3. **View job match scores**
   - Each job now displays a match score (percentage)
   - Jobs are automatically sorted by match score (highest first)
   - The match score indicates how well your skills align with the job requirements
   - **Expected Result**: You see personalized match percentages for each job

4. **Review matched skills**
   - Click on a job to view details
   - See which of your skills match the job requirements
   - View missing skills that the job requires
   - **Expected Result**: You understand why each job has its match score

5. **View skill recommendations**
   - Look for the "Recommended Skills" section
   - This shows skills that would improve your job matches
   - Skills are recommended based on jobs you're interested in
   - **Expected Result**: You get suggestions for skills to add to your profile

6. **Disable skill-based view**
   - To return to the standard view, toggle off skill-based matching
   - **Expected Result**: Jobs are displayed in standard order without match scores

### Task 7: Viewing Job Details

Access comprehensive information about a specific job posting.

1. **Select a job**
   - From the Job List, click on any job card or "View Details" button
   - **Expected Result**: A modal window or detail page opens with full job information

2. **Review job details**
   - The job details include:
     - Full job title and company name
     - Location and job type
     - Complete job description
     - Requirements and qualifications
     - Responsibilities
     - Experience level required
     - Skills required
     - Education requirements
     - Benefits and perks
     - Salary range (if provided)
     - Posted date
     - Application method (internal or external link)
   - **Expected Result**: You have all the information needed to evaluate the opportunity

3. **Check application eligibility**
   - For internal jobs, the system indicates if you can apply
   - For external jobs, you'll see a link to the external application page
   - **Expected Result**: You know how to proceed with the application

4. **Close the details**
   - Click the close button or outside the modal to return to the job list
   - **Expected Result**: You're back at the job list and can continue browsing

### Task 8: Accessing External Job Links

For external job postings, access the organization's application page.

1. **Identify external jobs**
   - External jobs are marked with "External Organization" as the source type
   - They display an "Apply on External Site" or similar button
   - **Expected Result**: You can distinguish external jobs from internal ones

2. **Click the external link**
   - In the job details, click the "Apply Now" or "View on External Site" button
   - **Expected Result**: A new browser tab opens with the external application page

3. **Complete the external application**
   - Follow the external organization's application process
   - The NORSU system does not track external applications
   - **Expected Result**: You're directed to the external site to apply

### Task 9: Combining Filters and Search

Use multiple filters and search together for precise results.

1. **Enter a search query**
   - Type keywords in the search box
   - **Expected Result**: Search terms are entered

2. **Apply filters**
   - Select a job type (e.g., "Full Time")
   - Select a source type (e.g., "NORSU Internal")
   - **Expected Result**: Multiple filters are active

3. **Enable skill matching (optional)**
   - Turn on skill-based view if desired
   - **Expected Result**: Skill matching is applied to filtered results

4. **Review combined results**
   - The job list shows only positions matching all your criteria
   - **Expected Result**: You see a highly targeted list of opportunities

5. **Clear filters individually or all at once**
   - Remove filters one by one or clear all to start over
   - **Expected Result**: You can refine your search iteratively

## Tips and Best Practices

- **Keep your profile updated**: Regularly update your skills, education, and experience for accurate skill matching
- **Use skill-based matching**: This feature helps you discover opportunities you might otherwise miss
- **Check regularly**: New jobs are posted frequently, so visit the job board often
- **Be specific in searches**: Use precise keywords to find exactly what you're looking for
- **Explore featured jobs**: Featured positions are often high-priority opportunities
- **Review match scores**: Even jobs with lower match scores might be worth exploring if you're willing to learn new skills
- **Act quickly**: Popular positions may fill fast, so apply promptly when you find a good match
- **Read full descriptions**: Always review the complete job details before applying
- **Note application deadlines**: Some jobs may have specific application windows

## Common Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Finding Remote Work Opportunities
Filter by job type "Remote" to see all positions that allow remote work. This is ideal for alumni living far from NORSU or seeking flexible work arrangements.

### Use Case 2: Discovering Jobs That Match Your Skills
Enable skill-based view to see which positions best align with your current qualifications. Focus on high-match jobs for better application success rates.

### Use Case 3: Exploring Career Change Options
Use skill matching to identify jobs with moderate match scores. Review the missing skills to understand what you'd need to learn for a career transition.

### Use Case 4: Finding Entry-Level Positions
Search for "entry level" or "junior" in the search box, or filter by experience level if available. This helps new graduates find appropriate opportunities.

### Use Case 5: Tracking NORSU Internal Opportunities
Filter by source type "NORSU Internal" to see positions within the NORSU network where you can apply directly through the system.

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: No Jobs Are Displayed
**Symptoms**: The job list is empty or shows "No jobs found"
**Solution**: 
- Check if you have any active filters or search terms that might be limiting results
- Clear all filters and search terms to view all jobs
- If still empty, there may be no active job postings at this time
- Contact administrators if you believe jobs should be visible

### Issue: Skill-Based Matching Not Working
**Symptoms**: Match scores don't appear or are all 0%
**Solution**:
- Ensure you have added skills to your profile
- Complete your education and work experience sections
- Refresh the page after updating your profile
- If the issue persists, contact system support

### Issue: Search Returns No Results
**Symptoms**: Your search query doesn't return any matching jobs
**Solution**:
- Try using different or more general keywords
- Check your spelling
- Clear the search and browse all jobs to see what's available
- Try searching by company name or location instead of job title

### Issue: External Job Link Not Working
**Symptoms**: Clicking an external job link doesn't open the application page
**Solution**:
- Check if your browser is blocking pop-ups
- Try right-clicking the link and opening in a new tab
- Copy the URL and paste it directly in your browser
- The external link may have expired - contact the posting organization

### Issue: Can't See Job Details
**Symptoms**: Clicking on a job doesn't show the full details
**Solution**:
- Ensure you're logged in to your account
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Clear your browser cache
- Try using a different browser

### Issue: Match Scores Seem Inaccurate
**Symptoms**: Skill match percentages don't reflect your qualifications
**Solution**:
- Review and update your profile skills
- Ensure skills are spelled correctly and match industry standards
- Add more detailed work experience and education information
- Match scores are calculated based on profile data - incomplete profiles yield lower scores

- Job postings are managed by HR staff and administrators

- Featured jobs are selected by administrators to highlight important opportunities

- Match scores are calculated based on skill overlap, education level, and experience
- External job links are provided as-is and may expire or change
