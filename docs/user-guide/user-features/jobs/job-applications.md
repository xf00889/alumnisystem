# Job Applications

## Overview

The Job Applications feature allows you to apply for NORSU internal job positions directly through the system. You can submit your application with required documents, track the status of your applications, and manage your job search process. The system provides a streamlined application workflow and keeps you informed about your application progress.

## Who Can Use This Feature

- **User Role**: All authenticated alumni users
- **Permissions Required**: Active user account
- **Prerequisites**: 
  - Must be logged in to the system
  - Complete profile with resume and relevant documents
  - Can only apply for NORSU Internal jobs or external jobs that accept internal applications

## How to Access

1. Log in to your alumni account
2. Navigate to the **Jobs** section
3. Find a job you want to apply for
4. Click on the job to view details
5. Click the **"Apply Now"** button

## Key Features

- Apply for NORSU internal positions through the system
- Upload required documents (resume, cover letter, etc.)
- Submit cover letters with your application
- Track application status (Pending, Shortlisted, Interviewed, Accepted, Rejected)
- View your application history
- Receive notifications about application status changes
- One application per job (prevents duplicate applications)
- Automatic validation of required documents

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Task 1: Viewing Job Details Before Applying

Before applying, review the complete job information to ensure it's a good fit.

1. **Navigate to the Job Board**
   - Click on **"Jobs"** in the main navigation menu
   - **Expected Result**: The Job List page is displayed

2. **Select a job to review**
   - Click on a job card or "View Details" button
   - **Expected Result**: The job details modal or page opens

3. **Review the job information**
   - Read the complete job description
   - Check the requirements and qualifications
   - Review the responsibilities
   - Note the required skills and experience level
   - Check the education requirements
   - Review benefits and salary range (if provided)
   - **Expected Result**: You have all information needed to decide if you want to apply

4. **Check if you can apply**
   - Look for the "Apply Now" button
   - Internal jobs show an application button
   - External jobs show a link to external site
   - If you've already applied, you'll see "Already Applied" status
   - **Expected Result**: You know whether you can apply through the system

### Task 2: Applying for an Internal Job

Submit your application for a NORSU internal position.

1. **Access the application form**
   - From the job details page, click the **"Apply Now"** button
   - **Expected Result**: The application form page opens

2. **Review the application requirements**
   - Check the list of required documents
   - Note any specific instructions
   - Ensure you have all necessary files ready
   - **Expected Result**: You know what documents you need to submit

3. **Fill out the application form**
   - The form includes:
     - **Cover Letter** (text area): Write a personalized cover letter explaining your interest and qualifications
     - **Resume/CV** (file upload): Upload your current resume (required)
     - **Additional Documents**: Upload any other required documents
   - **Expected Result**: All form fields are completed

4. **Upload your resume**
   - Click the **"Choose File"** button for the resume field
   - Select your resume file from your computer
   - Accepted formats typically include: PDF, DOC, DOCX
   - Maximum file size: Usually 5MB
   - **Expected Result**: Your resume is uploaded and the filename is displayed

5. **Upload required documents**
   - For each required document listed:
     - Click the corresponding **"Choose File"** button
     - Select the appropriate document from your computer
     - Ensure the document matches the requirement (e.g., transcript, certification)
   - **Expected Result**: All required documents are uploaded

6. **Write your cover letter**
   - In the cover letter text area, write a compelling introduction
   - Explain why you're interested in the position
   - Highlight relevant skills and experience
   - Keep it concise and professional (typically 250-500 words)
   - **Expected Result**: Your cover letter is complete and ready to submit

7. **Review your application**
   - Double-check all uploaded files
   - Review your cover letter for typos or errors
   - Ensure all required fields are completed
   - **Expected Result**: Your application is complete and accurate

8. **Submit the application**
   - Click the **"Submit Application"** button
   - **Expected Result**: A confirmation message appears indicating successful submission

9. **Confirmation**
   - You'll see a success message: "Your application has been submitted successfully!"
   - You're redirected back to the job details page
   - The "Apply Now" button is replaced with "Already Applied" status
   - **Expected Result**: Your application is submitted and you cannot apply again for this job

### Task 3: Tracking Application Status

Monitor the progress of your submitted applications.

1. **Access your applications**
   - Navigate to **"My Applications"** or **"Jobs"** section
   - Look for a link to view your application history
   - **Expected Result**: You see a list of jobs you've applied for

2. **View application status**
   - Each application displays its current status:
     - **Pending Review**: Your application has been submitted and is awaiting review
     - **Shortlisted**: You've been selected for further consideration
     - **Interviewed**: You've been interviewed for the position
     - **Accepted**: Your application has been accepted (congratulations!)
     - **Rejected**: Your application was not selected
   - **Expected Result**: You can see the status of each application

3. **Check application details**
   - Click on an application to view full details
   - Review:
     - Job title and company
     - Application date
     - Current status
     - Documents you submitted
     - Any notes or feedback (if provided)
   - **Expected Result**: You have complete information about your application

4. **Monitor for updates**
   - Check your applications regularly for status changes
   - You may receive email notifications when status changes
   - **Expected Result**: You stay informed about your application progress

### Task 4: Understanding Application Status Changes

Learn what each status means and what to expect next.

1. **Pending Review**
   - **What it means**: Your application has been received and is in the queue for review
   - **What to expect**: HR or hiring managers will review your application
   - **Timeline**: Review typically takes 1-2 weeks
   - **Action**: Wait for the next update; no action needed

2. **Shortlisted**
   - **What it means**: Your application has passed initial screening
   - **What to expect**: You may be contacted for an interview or additional information
   - **Timeline**: Contact typically within 1 week
   - **Action**: Ensure your contact information is up to date; prepare for potential interview

3. **Interviewed**
   - **What it means**: You've completed an interview for the position
   - **What to expect**: The hiring team is making final decisions
   - **Timeline**: Decision typically within 1-2 weeks after interview
   - **Action**: Wait for final decision; you may send a thank-you note

4. **Accepted**
   - **What it means**: Congratulations! You've been selected for the position
   - **What to expect**: You'll be contacted with next steps and offer details
   - **Timeline**: Immediate contact expected
   - **Action**: Respond promptly to any communications; prepare for onboarding

5. **Rejected**
   - **What it means**: Your application was not selected for this position
   - **What to expect**: No further action on this application
   - **Timeline**: N/A
   - **Action**: Continue applying for other opportunities; consider requesting feedback

### Task 5: Applying for External Jobs

Access external job opportunities that require applications on third-party sites.

1. **Identify external jobs**
   - External jobs are marked with "External Organization" as the source type
   - They display an external link icon or "Apply on External Site" button
   - **Expected Result**: You can distinguish external jobs from internal ones

2. **Review the job details**
   - Click on the external job to view full details
   - Read all information provided
   - Note the application link
   - **Expected Result**: You understand the job requirements

3. **Access the external application**
   - Click the **"Apply Now"** or **"View on External Site"** button
   - A new browser tab opens with the external organization's website
   - **Expected Result**: You're directed to the external application page

4. **Complete the external application**
   - Follow the external organization's application process
   - The NORSU system does not track external applications
   - You'll need to track these applications separately
   - **Expected Result**: You've submitted your application on the external site

5. **Keep your own records**
   - Note the job details and application date
   - Save any confirmation emails from the external site
   - Track the application status through the external organization's system
   - **Expected Result**: You maintain your own record of external applications

### Task 6: Preparing Application Documents

Ensure you have all necessary documents ready before applying.

1. **Update your resume**
   - Ensure your resume is current and tailored to the job
   - Save in an accepted format (PDF recommended)
   - Keep file size under the maximum limit (typically 5MB)
   - **Expected Result**: Your resume is ready to upload

2. **Prepare additional documents**
   - Common required documents include:
     - **Transcript of Records**: Official academic transcript
     - **Diploma**: Copy of your degree certificate
     - **Certifications**: Professional certifications or licenses
     - **Portfolio**: Work samples (for creative or technical roles)
     - **Recommendation Letters**: Letters from previous employers or professors
     - **Government ID**: Valid identification document
   - **Expected Result**: All required documents are prepared and accessible

3. **Write a draft cover letter**
   - Prepare a template cover letter
   - Customize it for each specific job
   - Keep it professional and concise
   - **Expected Result**: You have a cover letter ready to customize

4. **Organize your files**
   - Name files clearly (e.g., "John_Doe_Resume.pdf")
   - Keep all documents in an easily accessible folder
   - Ensure files are in the correct format
   - **Expected Result**: You can quickly access documents when applying

### Task 7: Handling Application Errors

Troubleshoot common issues during the application process.

1. **File upload fails**
   - Check file size (must be under maximum limit)
   - Verify file format is accepted
   - Try a different file format (e.g., convert DOC to PDF)
   - Ensure stable internet connection
   - **Expected Result**: File uploads successfully

2. **Missing required fields**
   - Review the form for any highlighted or marked fields
   - Complete all required fields before submitting
   - Check for error messages indicating what's missing
   - **Expected Result**: All required fields are completed

3. **Application already submitted**
   - You can only apply once per job
   - If you need to update your application, contact HR directly
   - Provide your application details and the updates needed
   - **Expected Result**: You understand the one-application-per-job policy

4. **Cannot access application form**
   - Ensure you're logged in to your account
   - Check if the job is still active and accepting applications
   - Verify the job is an internal position (external jobs redirect to external sites)
   - **Expected Result**: You can access the application form or understand why you cannot

## Tips and Best Practices

- **Tailor your application**: Customize your cover letter and resume for each specific job
- **Proofread carefully**: Check for spelling and grammar errors before submitting
- **Use professional documents**: Ensure your resume and documents are well-formatted and professional
- **Apply promptly**: Don't wait too long to apply for positions that interest you
- **Follow instructions**: Read and follow all application instructions carefully
- **Keep copies**: Save copies of your submitted applications and documents
- **Update your profile**: Keep your profile current to improve job matching
- **Be honest**: Provide accurate information in your application
- **Prepare for interviews**: If shortlisted, be ready for potential interviews
- **Follow up appropriately**: If you haven't heard back after a reasonable time, you may politely inquire about status

## Common Use Cases

### Use Case 1: First-Time Job Application
Review the job details thoroughly, prepare all required documents, write a compelling cover letter, and submit your application. Track the status regularly and be prepared for potential interviews.

### Use Case 2: Applying for Multiple Positions
Organize your documents in advance, customize your cover letter for each position, and keep track of all applications. Monitor status updates for each application separately.

### Use Case 3: Updating Application Materials
If you realize you need to update your application after submission, contact HR directly with your application details and the specific updates needed.

### Use Case 4: Following Up on Application Status
If your application has been in "Pending Review" for an extended period, you may politely contact HR to inquire about the timeline. Be professional and patient in your follow-up.

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Cannot Submit Application
**Symptoms**: The submit button doesn't work or shows an error
**Solution**:
- Ensure all required fields are completed
- Check that all required documents are uploaded
- Verify file sizes are within limits
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page and filling out the form again
- If the issue persists, contact system support

### Issue: File Upload Fails
**Symptoms**: Document upload doesn't complete or shows an error
**Solution**:
- Check file size (must be under maximum limit, typically 5MB)
- Verify file format is accepted (PDF, DOC, DOCX)
- Try converting the file to PDF format
- Ensure stable internet connection
- Try uploading from a different browser
- If the issue continues, contact support

### Issue: Already Applied Message
**Symptoms**: System says you've already applied but you don't remember applying
**Solution**:
- Check your application history to confirm
- You can only apply once per job posting
- If you need to update your application, contact HR directly
- If you believe this is an error, contact system administrators

### Issue: Application Status Not Updating
**Symptoms**: Your application status hasn't changed in a long time
**Solution**:
- Application review timelines vary by position
- Pending Review can take 1-2 weeks or longer
- If it's been more than 3 weeks, you may politely inquire with HR
- Check your email for any communications you may have missed
- Ensure your contact information in your profile is correct

### Issue: Cannot Access Application Form
**Symptoms**: Clicking "Apply Now" doesn't open the application form
**Solution**:
- Ensure you're logged in to your account
- Check if the job is still active
- Verify the job accepts internal applications (check source type)
- Clear your browser cache and try again
- Try using a different browser
- Contact support if the issue persists

### Issue: Cover Letter Text Not Saving
**Symptoms**: Your cover letter text disappears or doesn't submit
**Solution**:
- Write your cover letter in a text editor first, then copy and paste
- Avoid using special characters or formatting
- Ensure you don't exceed any character limits
- Don't navigate away from the page before submitting
- Save your cover letter externally as a backup
