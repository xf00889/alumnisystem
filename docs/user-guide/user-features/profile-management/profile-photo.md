# Profile Photo

## Overview

The Profile Photo feature allows you to upload, update, and manage your profile picture. A professional profile photo helps other alumni recognize you, makes your profile more engaging, and improves your visibility in the alumni network. Your photo appears throughout the system wherever your profile is displayed.

## Who Can Use This Feature

- **All authenticated users** with verified email addresses
- **Alumni who want to personalize their profile**
- **Users seeking to improve profile visibility**

## Prerequisites

- Active account with verified email
- Completed registration process
- Logged into the system
- Photo file in supported format (JPG, PNG, GIF)

## How to Access

1. Log into your NORSU Alumni System account
2. Navigate to your profile page
3. Click the "Edit Profile" button
4. Find the "Profile Photo" or "Avatar" section
5. Or visit: `/accounts/profile/update/`

## Key Features

- **Photo Upload**: Upload a profile picture from your device
- **Photo Preview**: See how your photo will look before saving
- **Photo Removal**: Remove your current photo and revert to default
- **Format Support**: Supports JPG, PNG, and GIF formats
- **Automatic Resizing**: System automatically optimizes photo size
- **Default Avatar**: System provides a default avatar if no photo is uploaded
- **Instant Update**: Photo updates immediately across the system

## Supported Photo Formats

- **JPEG/JPG**: Recommended for photos
- **PNG**: Good for images with transparency
- **GIF**: Supported but not recommended for profile photos

## Photo Requirements

### File Size
- Maximum file size: 5MB
- Recommended size: Under 1MB
- Larger files are automatically compressed

### Dimensions
- Minimum: 200x200 pixels
- Recommended: 400x400 pixels or larger
- Square photos work best
- System automatically crops to square

### Quality
- Clear, well-lit photo
- Face clearly visible
- Professional appearance
- No filters or heavy editing
- Recent photo (within last 2 years)


## Step-by-Step Guide

### Task 1: Uploading a Profile Photo

1. **Navigate to the profile edit page**
   - Go to your profile
   - Click "Edit Profile" button
   - **Expected Result**: Profile edit form is displayed

2. **Locate the profile photo section**
   - Find the "Profile Photo", "Avatar", or "Photo" section
   - Usually at the top of the form
   - **Expected Result**: Current photo or default avatar is displayed

3. **Click "Choose File" or "Upload Photo"**
   - Click the file selection button
   - **Expected Result**: File browser opens

4. **Select your photo**
   - Navigate to the photo location on your computer
   - Select the photo file (JPG, PNG, or GIF)
   - **Expected Result**: File is selected, filename appears

5. **Preview the photo**
   - System may show a preview of the selected photo
   - Check that the photo looks correct
   - **Expected Result**: Photo preview is displayed

6. **Adjust or crop (if available)**
   - Some systems allow you to crop or adjust the photo
   - Position your face in the center
   - **Expected Result**: Photo is adjusted as desired

7. **Save your changes**
   - Click "Save" or "Update Profile" button
   - Wait for upload to complete
   - **Expected Result**: Success message appears, photo is updated

8. **Verify the update**
   - Check your profile page
   - New photo should appear
   - Photo updates across the entire system
   - **Expected Result**: New photo is visible everywhere

### Task 2: Changing Your Profile Photo

1. **Navigate to profile edit page**
   - Go to your profile
   - Click "Edit Profile"
   - **Expected Result**: Edit form with current photo is displayed

2. **Click "Change Photo" or "Upload New Photo"**
   - Find the photo change option
   - **Expected Result**: File browser opens

3. **Select new photo**
   - Choose a different photo from your computer
   - **Expected Result**: New photo is selected

4. **Save the changes**
   - Click "Save" or "Update Profile"
   - **Expected Result**: Photo is updated successfully

### Task 3: Removing Your Profile Photo

1. **Navigate to profile edit page**
   - Go to your profile
   - Click "Edit Profile"
   - **Expected Result**: Edit form is displayed

2. **Locate the remove photo option**
   - Look for "Remove Photo", "Delete Photo", or a checkbox labeled "Remove avatar"
   - May be near the photo upload section
   - **Expected Result**: Remove option is visible

3. **Click "Remove Photo" or check the removal box**
   - Select the removal option
   - **Expected Result**: Removal is marked

4. **Save your changes**
   - Click "Save" or "Update Profile"
   - **Expected Result**: Photo is removed

5. **Verify removal**
   - Check your profile page
   - Default avatar should appear
   - **Expected Result**: Profile shows default avatar


## Tips and Best Practices

### Photo Selection
- **Use a Recent Photo**: Upload a current photo (within last 2 years)
- **Professional Appearance**: Dress professionally or business casual
- **Clear Face**: Ensure your face is clearly visible and well-lit
- **Neutral Background**: Use a plain or neutral background
- **Smile**: A friendly, approachable expression works best
- **Solo Photo**: Use a photo of just yourself, not a group
- **Good Lighting**: Natural light or well-lit indoor photos work best
- **Straight Angle**: Face the camera directly, avoid extreme angles

### Photo Quality
- **High Resolution**: Use a high-quality photo (at least 400x400 pixels)
- **In Focus**: Ensure the photo is sharp and not blurry
- **Proper Exposure**: Not too dark or too bright
- **No Filters**: Avoid heavy filters or excessive editing
- **Natural Colors**: Keep colors natural and realistic

### What to Avoid
- **Group Photos**: Don't use photos with multiple people
- **Sunglasses**: Remove sunglasses so your face is visible
- **Hats**: Avoid hats that obscure your face
- **Inappropriate Content**: No offensive or unprofessional images
- **Logos or Text**: Avoid photos with watermarks or text overlays
- **Selfies**: Professional photos are preferred over casual selfies
- **Old Photos**: Don't use photos from many years ago

### Technical Tips
- **Square Format**: Crop photos to square before uploading
- **Compress Large Files**: Reduce file size if over 1MB
- **Use JPG Format**: JPG is best for photos
- **Test on Mobile**: Check how photo looks on mobile devices
- **Update Regularly**: Change photo every 1-2 years

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: Photo upload fails
**Symptoms**: Upload button doesn't work or error message appears

**Solution**:
- Check file size (must be under 5MB)
- Verify file format (JPG, PNG, or GIF only)
- Check internet connection
- Try a different browser
- Compress the photo file
- Convert to JPG if using unsupported format
- Clear browser cache
- Contact support if issue persists

### Issue: Photo appears distorted or stretched
**Symptoms**: Photo looks stretched, squished, or cropped incorrectly

**Solution**:
- Use a square photo (same width and height)
- Crop photo to square before uploading
- Use minimum 400x400 pixels
- Try uploading a different photo
- Check photo orientation (should be upright)
- Contact support if distortion persists

### Issue: Photo is too large
**Symptoms**: "File size exceeds maximum" error

**Solution**:
- Compress photo using online tools
- Reduce image resolution
- Convert to JPG format (smaller than PNG)
- Crop unnecessary parts of the image
- Use photo editing software to reduce size
- Maximum size is typically 5MB


### Issue: Photo not updating
**Symptoms**: Upload succeeds but photo doesn't change

**Solution**:
- Refresh the page (Ctrl+F5 or Cmd+Shift+R)
- Clear browser cache
- Wait a few seconds for processing
- Check that you clicked "Save" after selecting photo
- Try a different browser
- Log out and log back in
- Contact support if photo still doesn't update

### Issue: Photo appears rotated
**Symptoms**: Photo is sideways or upside down

**Solution**:
- Rotate photo on your computer before uploading
- Use photo editing software to correct orientation
- Check photo metadata (EXIF data may cause rotation)
- Save photo in correct orientation
- Try uploading again

### Issue: Cannot remove photo
**Symptoms**: Remove button doesn't work or photo reappears

**Solution**:
- Ensure you checked the "Remove avatar" option
- Click "Save" after selecting remove
- Refresh the page
- Clear browser cache
- Try a different browser
- Contact support if removal fails

### Issue: Photo quality is poor after upload
**Symptoms**: Photo looks blurry or pixelated after uploading

**Solution**:
- Upload a higher resolution photo
- Use at least 400x400 pixels
- Ensure original photo is clear and in focus
- Avoid uploading already compressed images
- Use JPG format for best quality
- Don't upload screenshots of photos

- Good lighting and clear focus

- Recent photo (within 2 years)

### Acceptable Photos

## Where Your Photo Appears

Your profile photo is displayed in multiple places throughout the system:

- **Profile Page**: Main profile display
- **Alumni Directory**: Directory listings and search results
- **Messages**: Next to your messages in conversations
- **Comments**: On posts and comments you make
- **Connections**: In connection requests and lists
- **Job Applications**: On your job applications
- **Mentorship**: In mentor/mentee profiles
- **Groups**: In group member lists
- **Events**: In event attendee lists
- **Navigation**: In the top navigation bar when logged in

## Privacy and Visibility

### Photo Privacy
- Profile photo visibility follows your profile privacy settings
- If profile is public, photo is visible to all alumni
- If profile is private, photo is only visible to you and connections
- Administrators can always view your photo

### Changing Privacy
- Adjust profile privacy in Personal Information settings
- Photo privacy cannot be set separately from profile
- Consider privacy when choosing your photo


## Photo Editing Tips

### Before Uploading
1. **Crop to Square**: Use photo editing software to crop to 1:1 ratio
2. **Adjust Brightness**: Ensure photo is well-lit but not overexposed
3. **Remove Background Distractions**: Crop out unnecessary elements
4. **Check Resolution**: Verify photo is at least 400x400 pixels
5. **Save in JPG**: Convert to JPG format for best compatibility
6. **Compress if Needed**: Reduce file size if over 1MB

### Recommended Tools
- **Online**: Canva, Pixlr, Photopea
- **Desktop**: Adobe Photoshop, GIMP, Paint.NET
- **Mobile**: Snapseed, Adobe Lightroom Mobile, VSCO
- **Compression**: TinyPNG, Compressor.io, ImageOptim

### Quick Fixes
- **Too Dark**: Increase brightness and exposure
- **Too Bright**: Decrease exposure, adjust highlights
- **Wrong Size**: Crop to square, resize to 400x400 or larger
- **Wrong Format**: Save as JPG
- **Too Large**: Compress or reduce resolution
